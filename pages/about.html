About MHCS

Founded in Faith, Serving with Excellence

Our History

Founded in 1952, Mary Help of Christians Seminary has been a beacon of hope and faith for over seven decades. Beginning with a humble group of students and dedicated faculty, we have grown into a respected institution known for our commitment to excellence in spiritual and academic formation.

Our seminary stands on the foundation of Catholic values, encouraging students to become servant leaders in the Church and society. Over the years, MHCS has produced thousands of graduates who continue to make meaningful contributions to their communities.

Today, we remain committed to our founding principles while adapting to the contemporary needs of the Church and the world. Our legacy is built on the dedication of countless faculty members, staff, and alumni who have shaped our institution into what it is today.

Our Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To provide holistic, Christ-centered formation that develops spiritually mature, intellectually capable, and morally upright individuals committed to serving the Church and society.

Our Vision

A thriving seminary community producing pastorally sensitive, prophetic voices for the 21st century, rooted in the apostolic spirit of Mary, Help of Christians.

Formation Identity

How we form leaders for the Church and society

Spiritual Formation

Deep personal relationship with Christ through daily prayer, sacraments, spiritual direction, and community worship. We emphasize the development of a contemplative heart for active ministry.

Academic Excellence

Rigorous study of theology, philosophy, Scripture, and pastoral sciences. Our curriculum is designed to equip students with knowledge and skills for effective ministry in the contemporary world.

Human Development

Emphasis on psychological maturity, emotional intelligence, and healthy relationships. We support students in developing balanced personalities and strong leadership capabilities.

Pastoral Formation

Practical training in pastoral care, homiletics, counseling, and community engagement. Students gain real-world experience serving parishes and communities.

Community Life

Formation within a caring faith community that models collaboration, mutual support, and accountability. Community living is integral to our formative experience.

Justice & Mercy

Commitment to Catholic social teaching, preferential option for the poor, and advocacy for justice. We form agents of change who serve the marginalized and vulnerable.
